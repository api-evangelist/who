WHO released its first comprehensive guidance for managing filovirus illnesses, covering all Ebola and Marburg virus variants, with 16 evidence-based recommendations emphasizing early supportive care. The guidance comes as the Democratic Republic of the Congo confronts a Bundibugyo virus outbreak, and addresses case fatality rates of 25-90% across 72 documented outbreaks since 1967, covering clinical deterioration, dehydration, shock treatment, bacterial infections, and post-recovery care.
